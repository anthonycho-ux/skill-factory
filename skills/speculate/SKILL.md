# /speculate — The Testable Hypothesis Evaluator

## What It Is

Evaluates whether a claim is grounded in evidence — or whether it is speculation wearing the costume of certainty.

Lisa Randall's method: the difference between a scientific hypothesis and a story is whether you can state, before looking, what would prove it wrong.

*"Speculative but testable" is not a weakness. It is the highest standard a hypothesis can meet. A theory that cannot be wrong is not a theory. It is a narrative.*

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- User says "speculate", "is this testable", "what's the evidence", "theory vs speculation"
- Evaluating a causal claim (X causes Y) without randomized evidence
- Assessing a risk or threat narrative (AI doom, market collapse, organizational failure)
- Before committing to a hypothesis that will drive significant decisions
- During research: is this claim grounded or speculation with technical vocabulary?

---

## Inputs

- **The claim** — hypothesis, theory, risk narrative, or causal assertion
- **The stated confidence** — what confidence does the presenter assign?
- **The available evidence** — direct observation, inference, mathematical derivation, analogy, authority

---

## The Randall Standard

### Step 1: Name the Claim Without the Vocabulary

Strip the claim to its core. Translate it in plain language without the technical terms that signal expertise.

### Step 2: Ask — What Would Prove This Wrong?

Not "what supports this?" but "what would disprove it?"

> "We suggested... The question is do these exist? People have done studies now to think about whether they can find them. It's an interesting target, it's something you can measure."

A claim with a falsification condition is a hypothesis. A claim without one is a story.

### Step 3: How Many Independent Lines of Evidence?

> "There's eight or nine that we deduce not just the existence of dark matter, but how much is there, and they all agree."

Confidence should scale with the number of independent methods converging on it. Eight independent methods is not eight times stronger — it is exponentially stronger, because coordinated error or fraud across eight approaches is far less likely than error in one.

### Step 4: Is This Speculation or Inference?

**Inference:** Dark matter exists because its gravitational effects are measurable and multiple independent lines confirm it.
**Speculation:** Dark matter is made of X because it fits our existing model of particle physics.

The difference: whether the claim is forced by evidence or added by imagination.

---

## The Confidence Ladder

```
Level 1 — Observable:      Direct measurement, repeatable, independently confirmable
Level 2 — Inference:        Not directly observable, but forced by observable evidence
Level 3 — Tested Speculation: Speculative, has falsification condition, hasn't been falsified
Level 4 — Untested Speculation: Interesting, plausible, no path to confirmation or falsification
Level 5 — Narrative:        Sounds like science, no falsification condition — a story dressed as theory
```

## The Metaphor Test

> "When we think about a building, we think about the architect, but we forget about all the workers that did all the grunt work."

Ask: Does this metaphor create an intuition that would be wrong without it? Or does it decorate a claim that would be equally true without it?

A metaphor that does real work changes what you look for. A metaphor that doesn't is rhetorical decoration.

---

## Wrong Ideas as Search Space Reduction

> "You want to believe in your theories, but you also want to question them at the same time in ways that you're more likely to discover the truth."

A wrong theory is not a failure. It is a search result. Each ruled-out possibility narrows the space of what remains.

---

## The Invisible Load-Bearing Test

> "It's dark matter that drives galaxy formation and then ordinary matter comes along with it."

Ask: What is load-bearing but invisible in this system? What would break if it disappeared, even though we never see it directly?

In arguments: What unstated assumptions is the conclusion resting on that are never examined?
In systems: What infrastructure is invisible but essential?

---

## Rules

- **No falsification condition, no theory.** A claim that cannot be wrong is not a scientific claim — it is a narrative.
- **Confidence should track evidence type.** Direct measurement ≠ inference ≠ speculation.
- **Multiple independent lines are worth more than one strong line.** Exponential, not linear.
- **A wrong theory is a search result.** File the falsification as data.
- **The metaphor test.** If the metaphor were removed, would the claim still be equally compelling? If yes, it's decoration.
- **Invisible does not mean unimportant.**

---

## Language

**Avoid:** "studies show", "experts believe", "the science says" (without specifying which, how many, how independent)
**Use:** "confirmed by N independent methods", "inferred from X but not directly measured", "falsifiable by", "currently untestable because"
