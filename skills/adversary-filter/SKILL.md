# /adversary-filter — Test Utility Function Compatibility Before Extending Goodwill

## What It Is

Before entering any negotiation, partnership, or peace process with an adversary — apply the adversary filter. Ask whether the other party's utility function is compatible with your survival. If it is not, no amount of goodwill, economic interdependence, or rational argument will produce an agreement.

The engagement must be containment or elimination, not negotiation. Named from Douglas Murray's "death cult" filter: some actors operate from premises that do not overlap with yours.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Entering peace negotiations, ceasefires, or normalization processes
- Evaluating whether economic interdependence will constrain an adversary
- Assessing whether a counterparty's stated goals are compatible with yours
- Hearing "they're rational actors" without testing the claim
- Designing deterrence or containment strategies

---

## Steps

### 1. Apply the death cult filter.

Ask: is this actor's utility function compatible with my survival?
- **Compatible:** they want something I can give them that does not require my elimination
- **Incompatible:** their goals require my destruction, displacement, or subjugation as a precondition

If incompatible: stop. No negotiation is possible — only containment, elimination, or managed coexistence with overwhelming force.

> "There are actions that cannot be described as anything other than evil... unless you use the lexicon of evil somewhere."

The refusal to name incompatibility is not intellectual humility — it has consequences.

### 2. Apply the Norman Angell test.

Ask: is this actor primarily driven by rational self-interest?

Economic interdependence deters rational actors. It does not deter actors driven by ideology, grievance, theology, or apocalypse.

> "The idea that cooperation on an economic and other levels is any significant preventative device to madness breaking out is not something I see."

If their actions are costly to them and serve no material purpose, they are not a rational actor.

### 3. Map the pressure asymmetry.

Ask: who in this negotiation can actually be pressured?

> "America can put extraordinary pressure on Ukraine... but it's much easier to pressure Zelenskyy."

The pressure will flow to the most cooperative party by default. Before negotiating: map who can be pressured, who cannot, and whether the pressure asymmetry will produce an outcome that serves your actual interests.

### 4. Check the boring truth.

Ask: what is the obvious thing that no one is saying at the table?

> "The important thing is the only thing you do forget, everything else is what you remember."

The boring truth is obvious until everyone stops saying it. Then it becomes controversial. Then believing it makes you extreme. Before finalizing any agreement: state the boring truth.

### 5. Check the corruption signal.

Ask: what are the leaders at the top doing with their share?

> "Every Hamas leader, who's now dead, died a billionaire."

When leaders enrich themselves while demanding sacrifice from followers, the ideology is a front. Their consumption is evidence of their actual beliefs, not their stated ones.

---

## Rules

- **Do not extend goodwill to actors who cannot reciprocate it.** Assuming you want peace does not mean your adversary does.
- **Do not assume rational actor models apply to irrational actors.** The Norman Angell error is always available and always catastrophic when it fails.
- **The boring truth costs social capital to say.** If it did not, it would already have been said.
- **Corruption at the top is diagnostic, not incidental.**
- **Pressure asymmetry is structural.** It does not resolve with better intentions.
