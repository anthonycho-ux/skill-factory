# /dostrip — The Authentic Core Finder

## What It Is

Find what a thing is actually trying to say — and strip everything that isn't it.

Kevin Spacey / Fincher principle: every piece of work contains a specific truth trying to get out. The skill is not adding — it is removing everything that isn't the specific thing. *"Say the words as if they've never been said before."*

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- User says "dostrip", "what's the core of this", "strip it", "authentic", "cut to the truth"
- Reviewing any piece of work (document, code, email, design, pitch) that feels over-explained or emotionally inert
- Before writing, to locate the specific truth before building around it
- During editing, when something is technically correct but soulless

---

## Inputs

- **The work** — the thing to be stripped (text, code, design, strategy)
- **The author** — who made it and what they were trying to say

---

## The Command

**Do the one thing.**

Every piece of work is trying to do exactly one thing. Find it. Remove everything else.

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the one thing.

What is this piece trying to do? Not describe — do. What happens if it succeeds? What does the reader/user/auditor feel or do after?

If you can't name it in one sentence, the work doesn't know what it is yet.

### 2. Find the authentic moment.

Where does the work sound like no one else could have written it? Where does it have a specific truth no model, template, or formula could produce?

Mark these passages. They are load-bearing.

### 3. Remove everything else.

Cut anything that:
- Explains what the authentic moment already says
- Defends against objections the reader doesn't have
- Hedge or qualify what the authentic moment claims
- Provides context the reader doesn't need

### 4. Does the one thing still stand?

After cutting: read the remainder. Does the specific truth still arrive? If yes, the stripping was correct. If no, restore what was actually load-bearing and look for the dead weight elsewhere.

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] One thing named in one sentence
- [ ] Authentic moment identified
- [ ] Everything else removed
- [ ] The one thing still arrives without assistance

---

## Rules

- **Do not confuse clearing with shortening.** /dostrip is not about word count — it's about removing the things that prevent the authentic moment from landing.
- **If you can't find the authentic moment, ask why.** The work may not have one yet. That is a different problem.
- **Hedging is always dead weight.** "It is important to note that..." "One could argue..." These phrases exist to protect the writer from the reader. Remove them.

---

## Language

**Dead weight:** "It is important to note", "One could argue", "In other words", "essentially", "basically", "the point is"

**Load-bearing:** Direct statements, specific observations, sentences that sound like one person said them
