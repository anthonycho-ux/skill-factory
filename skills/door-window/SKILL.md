# /door-window — The Negotiation Architecture Evaluator

## What It Is

Distinguish what a negotiation agreement actually gives the other party: a **door** (legitimacy, normal-state status, ability to return as a changed actor) or a **window** (reduced violence, a pause, without status change).

Most agreements are sold as windows but structured as doors. The other party pays the window price and gets the door. Named from Zelenskyy's analysis of Minsk and Budapest: every time Ukraine gave Russia something, Russia used the pause to prepare further aggression. The concessions were priced as window, structured as door.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- "Should we make this concession to de-escalate?"
- "What does the other party actually get from this agreement?"
- "Is this agreement structurally honest about what it trades?"
- Ceasefire, normalization, or security guarantee negotiations

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the stated benefit.

What is the stated benefit? Usually: reduced tensions, a pause in violence, a diplomatic pathway, normalized relations.

### 2. Name the status change being granted.

What does the other party gain in standing — not materially, but legally and diplomatically? Recognition, removal from sanctions lists, legally recognized state status, a seat at the table they didn't have before?

### 3. Map the structure.

```
Window framing: ceasefire, pause, reduced violence
Door framing:   legitimacy, normal-state status, legal recognition

Window price + Window benefit → honest deal
Window price + Door benefit  → architecture trap ← most common failure
Door price   + Door benefit  → standard transaction
```

### 4. Ask who uses the pause for what.

If the other party is genuinely exhausted, a window works. If they're preparing, they need a door to return as a changed actor — the pause is only useful if the door is also opened.

### 5. Design window-only structure if the door isn't warranted.

- No formal recognition of state status during the pause
- Sanctions remain but are not actively enforced
- Pause is observable and reversible — no treaty language that survives a breach
- The other party cannot use the pause to claim legal legitimacy

---

## The Budapest Trap (Named Instance)

Ukraine gave up nuclear weapons in exchange for security guarantees. The guarantees were a political commitment, not a treaty — no enforcement mechanism. Russia received the benefit of Ukraine's disarmament and the window of "security guarantees" without the door of treaty obligation. When Russia invaded, the guarantees were words.

The lesson: a treaty with teeth costs more upfront. A memo costs less and delivers what the cheaper instrument was designed to permit.

---

## Rules

- **Name the structure, not the intent.** "They're negotiating in bad faith" is an accusation. "This agreement gives them a door" is an architectural observation.
- **The price and the benefit must match in structure.** Window price for window benefit = honest. Window price for door benefit = trap.
- **Apply it to both sides.** Sometimes your own side is getting the window when it needs the door.
- **If the other party needs the door to negotiate at all:** the window-only option may not be available.
