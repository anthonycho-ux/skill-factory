# /no-explain — The Demonstration Integrity Evaluator

## What It Is

If a demonstration requires narration, it is not yet impressive enough to stand on its own. Strip the explanation and show what the thing actually does. The work must be worth showing before it is shown.

*"My theory of the video is no explanation. If they can't see it, then it's not the right thing."*

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Preparing a demo, presentation, or demonstration
- The explanation is doing more work than the thing itself
- You catch yourself wanting to add a narration layer ("what you're seeing here is...")
- Building a showcase, launch artifact, or public-facing demonstration
- Testing whether the work is genuinely compelling

---

## Steps

### 1. Watch the demo with all narration removed.

Close your eyes. What can you perceive directly? Movement, outcome, beauty, power, speed, resolution of a problem — what is visible without being told?

If the answer is "not much," the demonstration is not doing the work. The explanation is the evidence.

### 2. Name what remains when narration is stripped.

The thing that survives silence is the actual demonstration. Is it:
- A system doing something the viewer cannot do themselves?
- A before/after that is physically legible?
- An adversarial test the viewer can see failing and succeeding?
- A property (elegance, speed, force, delicacy) perceptible without context?

If what remains is thin, the demo is a narration wrapper over an insufficient demonstration.

### 3. Ask whether the thing needs the explanation, or the explanation is the thing.

Would the thing be more impressive if you just showed it to a stranger and said nothing?

- Yes: cut the narration and show it.
- No: the narration is context, not compensation.

### 4. Apply to failure modes too.

Raibert showed robot falls in the same video as robot successes — no explanation, no apology. The failure is the argument for why the success is hard. Showing both without narration is the strongest demonstration form: the viewer decides what to notice, and what they notice is real.

---

## Rules

- **Remove the narration, then evaluate.** The narration is not neutral — it tells the viewer what to feel.
- **Complexity is not an exemption.** Complex things must also be shown without narration.
- **Failure modes count as demonstrations.** Showing the robot falling is not a liability — it is the evidence.
- **If it requires a slide, it requires more work.** Fix the demonstration, not the slide.

---

## Chaining

```
Is this ready to demo?         → /no-explain
Is this version good enough?   → /no-explain (if it needs explaining, it needs fixing)
Show it compellingly?          → /no-explain then /dostrip for the core message
```
