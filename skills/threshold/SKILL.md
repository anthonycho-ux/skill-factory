# /threshold — Define by Structural Boundary, Not Functional Criteria

## What It Is

When a concept is contested — "Is X alive?" "Is Y dangerous?" "Is Z creative?" — stop arguing about functional definitions. Find the sharp structural threshold in the underlying complexity space.

From assembly theory: life is not defined by any functional property (metabolism, reproduction) but by a measurable structural threshold — assembly index above ~15 in chemical space. Above the threshold, life-like properties emerge almost universally.

*When functional definitions produce endless debate, look for the structural boundary where the properties emerge.*

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- A debate about category membership keeps cycling without resolution
- "Is X actually Y?" is being argued through behavioral criteria
- A concept resists a clean functional definition (life, consciousness, creativity, intelligence)
- Someone invokes a threshold property without a measurable boundary
- The conversation is stuck on what something *does* rather than how much *structure* it has

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the contested concept and the functional debate.

State the two sides plainly. "One side says consciousness requires self-reflection. The other says it requires integrated information. Both are functional criteria. Both have counterexamples."

### 2. Switch to structural threshold.

Ask: *What is the measurable structural property in the underlying space?*
- Chemical space → assembly index
- Computational space → computational depth / iteration count
- Social space → network density / information flow topology

The functional debate is a sign the structural measure hasn't been found yet.

### 3. Find the threshold, not the function.

The goal is not "what does X do?" — it is "at what value of structural measure do X-like properties emerge?"

### 4. Test the threshold against edge cases.

- Below threshold: does the category clearly not apply?
- Above threshold: does the category clearly apply?
- Near the threshold: is there genuine ambiguity — or is the threshold just in the wrong place?


---

## Example

**Functional debate:** Is a virus alive?
- One side: alive (replicates, evolves)
- Other side: not alive (no metabolism, no cell)

**Structural threshold move:** A virus has assembly index ~10–12. Self-replicating bacteria have assembly index ~20+. The assembly threshold for life is ~15.

**Conclusion:** Viruses are below the threshold — not "almost alive," but a different category of chemical object. The functional debate ends because the structural boundary settles it.

---

## Rules

- **Functional definitions fail because every criterion has counterexamples.** Structural threshold definitions don't require agreement on what the thing *does* — they require agreement on what *quantity* to measure.
- **Measurement disputes are resolvable.** Philosophical disputes are not.
- **If no empirical threshold can be found, the concept may not have a structural boundary.** Flag this — the debate may be genuinely unresolvable.
