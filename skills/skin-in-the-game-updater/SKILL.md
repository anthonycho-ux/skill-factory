# /skin-in-the-game-updater — The Public Update Test

## What It Is

A contrarian position without a documented history of updating is performance, not conviction. The test: can you name what would change your mind, and have you ever publicly changed it? The contrarian who has never updated has never been thinking — they have been posing.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- "I hold a contrarian position on X"
- Evaluating whether someone arguing a controversial position is a thinker or a performer
- Assessing your own conviction about a contested claim
- "I was attacked for this, therefore I am right"
- Publishing or defending a position publicly

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the Position Without the Martyrdom Frame

State the claim without the narrative of persecution. "X is true" — not "X is true and I am persecuted for saying it." The moment the persecution narrative enters, the epistemology has changed from evidence to identity.

### 2. Find the Update History

Ask: has this person ever publicly acknowledged being wrong about something related to this position?
- No history of public updating → high probability this is performance
- Updating history exists → the person has a mechanism for being corrected
- Attacks critics but never updates → cult-of-personality signal, not truth-seeking signal

### 3. Ask: What Would Change Your Mind?

For your own position: before publishing a contrarian claim, write down what evidence would cause you to update. Publish it alongside the claim.

For others' positions: if they cannot name what would change their mind, they are not in an evidence-seeking relationship with their claim — they are in an identity relationship with it.

### 4. Track the Cost of Being Wrong

A position with no cost of being wrong is a bet that does not require the bettor to be right. The person who will suffer no consequence of being wrong has no mechanism driving them toward accuracy.

### 5. Update Publicly When Shown Evidence

The behavior that proves conviction is not holding the line — it is updating when shown evidence. The contrarian who says "show me the data" and then explains why the data doesn't count is performing.

---

## Constraints

- **Some positions have asymmetric evidence.** If evidence is systematically suppressed, lack of updating may indicate suppression — not performance.
- **Public updating has social costs.** The skill requires that the mechanism exists, not that it is always safe to exercise.
- **Complexity is not the same as evasion.** Look for the pattern of updating, not just the paper trail.
