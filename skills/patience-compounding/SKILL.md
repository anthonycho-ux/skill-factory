# /patience-compounding — Long-Horizon Conviction Under Criticism

## What It Is

Interrupts reactive responses to external criticism and re-aligns decision-making to long-horizon trajectory data. Use when public perception diverges from internal progress and the pressure to change direction is highest.

Sundar Pichai pattern: *"I knew even through moments like that last year, I had a good sense of what we were building internally."*

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Facing criticism that your strategy is wrong while executing a multi-year bet
- Public narrative diverges sharply from internal trajectory
- External pressure to reverse a structural decision
- The gap between press cycle and product cycle is widest

---

## Inputs

- **The criticism** — what is being said and by whom
- **Internal trajectory data** — metrics, model curves, team progress (if available)

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Signal Audit

Ask: *What is the external criticism, and what internal data do I have that confirms or contradicts it?*

- If internal data contradicts the criticism → continue
- If internal data confirms the criticism → course-correct from data, not press
- If no internal data exists → fill that gap before reacting to anything

---

### Step 2 — Consequential Sorting

Ask: *Is this criticism aimed at one of my few consequential decisions, or the majority of my operational ones?*

Most criticism targets the 90% of visible surface area that doesn't matter. The 10% that does is structurally invisible to outsiders.

- Consequential (structural) decision → engage seriously
- Operational or aesthetic → note and move on

---

### Step 3 — Hear Everyone Out, Then Decide

Before the decision: gather dissent. After hearing: state conviction calmly.

Sequence: **data → dissent → deliberation → conviction → communicate**
Not: conviction → justify

---

### Step 4 — Patience as Structure, Not Personality

When organizational criticism involves team or structural decisions:

Ask: *Am I rushing toward visible "synergy" or letting the natural structure emerge?*

Elite team mergers require more time, not less. Slowness is not failure — it is correct calibration.

---

### Step 5 — Velocity Metric Check

Ask: *Am I measuring what compounds, or what flatters?*

Vanity: "30% of code is AI-generated."
Velocity: "10% engineering velocity increase."

Measure compounding rate, not current output. Velocity leads; output lags.

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Decision driven by internal trajectory data, not external pressure
- [ ] Consequential vs. operational sorting applied explicitly
- [ ] Conviction stated after dissent heard
- [ ] Patience chosen as structural variable

---

## Rules

- **Do not use when internal data genuinely contradicts the strategy.** This skill handles perception gaps — not genuine execution failures.
- **Pressure is not information.** The moment of maximum external criticism is often the moment of maximum internal progress.
- **The press cycle is not the product cycle.** They operate on different time horizons and measure different things.
