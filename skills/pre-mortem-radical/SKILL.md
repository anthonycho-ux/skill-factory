# /pre-mortem-radical — Own the Worst Before They Do

## What It Is

Before committing to or presenting a decision, write the opposing case first — the strongest possible attack on your own position. Acknowledge your flaws proactively.

The goal: defuse the opponent's weapon by owning it yourself. This is the James Sexton / "8 Mile strategy" — in a deposition, the witness who volunteers their worst moment on direct examination takes all the power out of the cross.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- "I'm about to decide on X"
- "I need to present X to..."
- "I need to defend X"
- "What's the strongest version of the argument against my position?"

---

## Inputs

- **Decision** — the choice you're about to make or present
- **Audience** — who will challenge this (adversarial, skeptical, or neutral)

---

## Phase 1 — Internal Pre-Mortem

1. State the decision in one sentence.
2. Write the strongest possible case **against** your position. Not a weakman — the steelman of the opposition.
3. Identify the 2–3 facts or arguments that would most damage you if voiced by someone hostile.
4. Ask: **Would I rather surface this myself, or have it surfaced against me?**

---

## Phase 2 — Own It

5. For each weakness: write a brief, honest acknowledgment.
   - Bad: "I know this looks bad..."
   - Good: "Yes — [conceded fact]. Here's why [it doesn't defeat the conclusion]."
6. Preempt the worst interpretation. State it before it is stated at you.
7. Reframe the weakness: does it reveal a constraint, a trade-off, or a value? Use it.

---

## Phase 3 — Decide or Present

8. If the acknowledgment changes the decision: update the decision.
9. If the acknowledgment doesn't defeat the decision: proceed with credibility intact.
10. If presenting to others: lead with the acknowledgment, not the argument.

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Opposition's strongest attack has already been surfaced and addressed
- [ ] No "gotcha" remaining that could emerge unexpectedly
- [ ] Credibility preserved or strengthened by the acknowledgment
- [ ] Decision holds after the worst version is on the table

---

## Rules

- **Do not perform this as theater.** Hedging without owning defeats the purpose.
- **The acknowledgment must be genuine.** Fake vulnerability is transparent.
- **Most useful in adversarial contexts** — negotiations, debates, proposals reviewed by skeptics.
- **Lead with the wound, not the defense.** If you're going to get hit, be the one who hits yourself first.

---

## Fuzzy Areas

- At what point does radical owning-it become self-sabotage? Calibration signal needed.
- Group settings where one person "owns it" on behalf of a team may need a sub-mode.

---

## Source

James Sexton — Lex Fridman Podcast #396 (divorce lawyer, trial veteran)

"8 Mile strategy": the witness who volunteers their worst moment on direct examination takes all the power out of the cross. The cross-examiner's job is to make you look bad — if you've already done it yourself, they have nowhere to go.
