# /totalwar — Simultaneous Multi-Front Pressure

## What It Is

Before launching a reform in a system — map all axes of resistance and coordinate pressure across them simultaneously. Single-axis reform in a multi-axis system is almost always surrounded and dissolved by the surrounding system.

The principle from Milei: Education, media, culture, economics move as a system. Change one without the others and the others close ranks.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Attempting to reform, replace, or change something in a system where other institutions are not aligned
- "We'll fix this one piece and the rest will follow"
- A change is being resisted by something that isn't the direct opponent
- Someone is winning the argument but losing the war
- Keywords: "reform", "institutional change", "disrupt", "compete them out of existence"

---

## Steps

### 1. Map all axes of the system.

Name them explicitly: institutional, cultural, economic, media/narrative, personnel. What are they? Who controls them? What do they want to preserve?

### 2. Which axis are you changing?

Name it precisely. This is where the visible effort is concentrated.

### 3. Which axes will resist?

The resistance is never just the direct opponent. It is the surrounding system adapting to preserve itself. When you change education without changing media, media frames the educated.

### 4. Is your effort coordinated across all necessary axes?

*"Is this a single-axis operation in a multi-axis system?"* Single-axis reform almost always fails. The other axes close ranks.

### 5. If single-axis: reform or build parallel?

**Reform:** bring the other axes along slowly, accepting the captured system will fight back on every front.
**Build parallel:** extract the credential, do the work elsewhere, let the institution become irrelevant by proving the alternative superior. Mises and Hayek didn't reform the academy — they built outside it until the academy had to respond.

---

## Rules

- **Single-axis reform is slow-motion failure.** The surrounding system has more mass than your one axis.
- **The credential opens the door.** Milei uses Harvard credentials to get hearings, then deploys Austrian school economics that Harvard doesn't teach.
- **Institutional capture is reversible — but not through reform alone.** Build parallel capacity. Prove the alternative.
- **"Win the argument, lose the war" is the signature failure mode.** The idea wins when it has an army.

---

## Fuzzy Areas

- **Scale matters.** Personal habit change, organizational change, and political reform all follow the same principle with very different execution mechanics.
- **When is reform possible vs. parallel necessary?** Strong cultural alignment + weak institutional capture = reform. Strong institutional capture + weak cultural alignment = build parallel. Both strong = total war.
