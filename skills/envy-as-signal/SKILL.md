# /envy-as-signal — The Envy Inversion

## What It Is

Envy is information, not pathology. When you feel envious of someone, the feeling is pointing you toward what you want. The standard response — suppression, comparison, resentment — wastes the signal. The inversion: use envy as a diagnostic tool for desire clarity.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Feeling envious of someone and not knowing why
- Trying to identify what you actually want
- Comparing yourself to someone else and coming away feeling deficient
- "I shouldn't feel this way about their success"
- Choosing between competing goals and needing to know which one you really want

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the Envy Without Judgment

State exactly what you are envious of. Not "I'm a bad person for feeling this" — just: "I am envious of their career / their output / their freedom." Name the object precisely.

### 2. Extract the Want

Ask: what does this tell me I actually want?
- Not "I want what they have" — that is too vague
- "I want the kind of recognition they have"
- "I want to be known for producing work like that"
- "I want to feel that kind of freedom in my schedule"

The envy is a compressed statement of desire. Decompress it.

### 3. Check for Impossibility Belief

Ask: why do I think I can't have it?
- "I don't have their talent"
- "It's too late for me"
- "They had opportunities I don't have"

These beliefs are the real problem. They are beliefs, not facts.

### 4. Convert the Gap to a Todo List

The gap between you and them is:
- A list of skills you need to develop
- A set of constraints you need to remove
- A direction you need to head in

The gap is not a verdict. It is a specification.

### 5. Practice Gratitude Separately — Not as a Substitute

Gratitude for what you have is real and important. It is not a substitute for clarity about what you want. Do both: be grateful for where you are AND clear about where you want to go.

---

## Constraints

- **Envy of intrinsic traits (looks, talent) is harder to convert.** You can develop skill; you cannot acquire everything envied.
- **Envy can be manufactured.** Social media highlight reels create envy for things not actually desirable for you. Verify the want before acting on it.
- **Resentment is different from envy.** Resentment is envy that has been suppressed and turned into a story about unfairness. This skill applies to raw envy, not the secondary emotion.

## Source

Jordan Peterson — Lex Fridman Podcast #448

Core observation: "The only reason you're envious is because you see someone who has something that you want. The fact that they have it means that in principle, you could get it." Envy as directional signal, not character defect.
