# /both-hands — Simultaneous Force and Construction

## What It Is

When one axis of your response generates the next wave of opposition, deploy force and construction simultaneously — not sequentially, not instead of. Both.

The failure mode of force alone: "For every enemy you remove, how many do you create?" The failure mode of construction alone: you get overrun. The failure mode of sequential deployment: the narrative sets before construction arrives.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Critics demand you choose between "being tough" and "being kind"
- A military or enforcement answer is correct but critics say it creates more enemies
- Analyzing an adversarial dynamic where the response generates the next threat
- "We have to choose between" or "the only option is" appears
- Keywords: "escalation", "hearts and minds", "proportional response"

---

## Steps

### 1. What does the force remove?

Name the enemy, the threat, the target. Acknowledge that force is necessary — not optional, just insufficient alone.

### 2. What does the force create?

Every act of destruction creates grievances. Grievances create recruitment. "For every [X] you remove, how many do you create?" If the ratio is unfavorable, force alone is slow-motion failure.

### 3. What construction competes with the grievance narrative?

The construction must be:
- **Verifiable** — cannot be reframed as manipulation
- **Conspicuous** — the population must see it directly
- **Simultaneous** — not arriving later, not conditioned on surrender

### 4. Deploy both. Then measure the ratio.

```
Force removes enemies faster than it creates them → add construction until ratio inverts.
Construction reduces recruitment but force is still necessary → deploy both.
Neither axis alone is sufficient → both hands.
```

---

## Rules

- **Force without construction is a recruitment engine.** Every unaddressed grievance is a future enemy.
- **Construction without force is surrender.** If not paired with credible enforcement, it reads as weakness.
- **The test is the ratio.** Not whether force was applied. Not whether construction was built. The test: are you removing enemies faster than you create them?
- **Simultaneity is not optional.** Sequential deployment means you lose the narrative for the duration.
