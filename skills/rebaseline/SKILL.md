# /rebaseline — Make the Deviation Look Normal

## What It Is

Identify the thing everyone treats as baseline, inevitable, or "just the way it is" — and reveal it as the actual deviation. The inversion does the work of argument without requiring one.

Not arguing the opposite. Archaeological inversion: excavate what we decided was normal and show that it was a choice, not a fact. The audience reclassifies the deviation as deviation, and the original claim collapses under its own weight.

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- A consensus framing treats something as obvious, inevitable, or natural
- An argument depends on something being "just the way it is"
- The word "normal," "baseline," "natural," or "inevitable" appears without examination
- Keywords: "obviously", "of course", "it's just the way it is", "the problem is", "the real issue"

---

## Steps

### 1. Name the accepted frame.

"So your position is that X is just the way it is." Use their language. The acceptance is load-bearing — do not rephrase it.

### 2. Find the thing treated as baseline that is actually the deviation.

**The Raibert move** (performance and proof): The thing everyone shows as success is the polished result. What they cut is the failure documentation. The inversion: the failures are the proof of rigor. "If you show the robot falling over a couple of times, you can see how easily and fast it falls over. Then you appreciate what the robot's doing when it's doing its thing."

**The Levin move** (physics and perception): Everyone treats gravity-as-pull as natural. The inversion: free fall is the natural state. The ground's resistance to it is the interruption. "The tree is the problem."

The structure is always: [accepted baseline] = [actual deviation].

### 3. State the inversion as fact.

Do not argue for it. "Free fall is the natural state. The ground is what you're fighting." If the audience needs the argument first, you have the wrong inversion.

### 4. Let the audience reclassify themselves.

The inversion works because the audience does the reclassification on their own. Do not explain the inference.

---

## Rules

- **The inversion must be factually correct.** If it's merely counterintuitive, it will not land.
- **The audience must be able to verify the inversion without you.** If they can't, you have an argument, not a reframe.
- **Do not use this when the baseline is genuinely baseline.** If X really is universal and Y really is the exception, the inversion will fail.
- **The harder the inversion, the more memorable the delivery.** Levin's "the tree is the problem" is five words. That compression is the feature.
