# /commission-not-omission — The Regret Decoder

## What It Is

Reframe regret from errors of omission toward errors of commission — then extract diagnostic signal from what you actively chose.

Errors of omission sting. Errors of commission teach. The question is never "should I regret this?" — it is "what did betting on this teach me about my model of the world?"

---

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- "I missed an opportunity / passed on a bet / made a decision that didn't pan out. Should I regret it?"
- Reviewing past decisions and cataloging things you didn't do
- A decision went wrong and you're second-guessing the process

---

## Inputs

- **The decision** — what you did or didn't do
- **The outcome** — what actually happened

---

## Step 1 — Classify the Error

Ask: *Did I actively bet on this and lose? Or did I decline to bet?*

**Commission error** (you bet and lost): → Go to Step 2
**Omission error** (you passed): → Go to Step 3

---

## Step 2 — Commission Error Protocol

You bet. You lost. The diagnostic question:

**What did winning look like in my model, and how was the actual outcome different?**

The gap between your model and reality is signal. Not "was I right or wrong" — but "what did my model miss that this outcome revealed?"

Four possible readings of the gap:
- **Bad luck** — model was sound, outcome was variance. No change needed.
- **Unknown unknowns** — something you didn't know you didn't know. Add to the model.
- **Model was wrong** — the world works differently than you thought. Update.
- **You didn't actually believe your model** — you bet without conviction. That is a different problem.

---

## Step 3 — Omission Error Protocol

You passed. Nothing happened. The diagnostic question:

**What would you have learned if you'd bet?**

If the answer is "nothing useful" → the omission was correct. Stop regretting.
If the answer is "I'd know whether X actually works" → the omission was fear. Note it and decide whether to bet now.
If the answer is "I'd have learned something about myself" → that is always worth knowing. Go bet.

---

## Step 4 — The Decision

After classification:

**Commission error with a wrong model** → Update the model. Regret the gap, not the bet.
**Commission error with bad luck** → No regret. Repeat the bet.
**Omission error from fear** → Go bet.
**Omission error from correctly calculated risk** → No regret. Let it go.

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Error classified correctly (commission vs. omission)
- [ ] Gap between model and outcome stated precisely
- [ ] Reading of the gap selected (luck / unknown unknowns / wrong model / no conviction)
- [ ] Decision: update, repeat, go bet, or let go

---

## Rules

- **Errors of omission are not diagnosable.** You don't know what would have happened. You only know what you feared. Fear is not data.
- **The regret is not the point.** What you learn from the regret is the point.
- **"I didn't know" is not a verdict — it's a starting point.** Unknown unknowns are the most valuable discovery.
