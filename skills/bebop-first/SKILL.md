# /bebop-first — Absorption Before Analysis

## What It Is

In complex domains, fluency precedes understanding — you absorb the language of a practice before you can articulate its grammar. Bebop musicians learned to play before they learned theory. Apply this when the standard approach (theory first, application second) is producing shallow results.

## Trigger

Invoke when:
- Learning a complex domain and hitting a ceiling with analytical study
- Teaching a domain and seeing students who "know" but can't perform
- "Why do I know this but can't do it?"
- Deciding the learning sequence for a new domain

## Inputs

- **Domain** — the skill or body of knowledge being acquired
- **Current stage** — theoretical study, early practice, deep repetition
- **Gap** — what you can articulate vs. what you can execute

## Steps

### 1. Identify the Language

What is the native texture of this domain before it has a grammar? For music: the sound of the thing. For code: reading and writing before abstraction. For judgment: exposure to cases before frameworks.

Name what exists before the theory.

### 2. Flood Before You Explain

Massive exposure without analysis. Listen, read, imitate, copy, run. Do not introduce the framework until the student has accumulated a felt sense of what the thing is supposed to look and feel like.

The goal: build an internal reference library. You can only reverse-engineer what you can recognize.

### 3. Let Analysis Follow Fluency

Once the reference library exists, analysis becomes generative — it explains what you already feel. The student says "oh, THAT'S why it sounds like that" rather than "I understand the rule but it sounds wrong."

If analysis changes the output rather than explaining it, you are in the wrong order.

### 4. Verify With Execution, Not Recall

The test of absorption: not "can you describe it" — but "can you do it without thinking." Can you produce the right output under cognitive load, without the analytical system running.

If the student can pass the theory exam but not the performance test, absorption hasn't happened yet.

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Massive exposure phase completed before theory is introduced
- [ ] Analysis produces "oh THAT'S why" moments, not "so I should"
- [ ] Execution under load matches or exceeds analytical performance
- [ ] Internal reference library exists for the domain

## Constraints

- **Not all domains benefit from this.** Sequential/linear domains (basic math, formal logic) may not have a "language" phase.
- **Flooding requires good inputs.** Absorbing the wrong language builds the wrong library.
- **Risk of shallow imitation.** Flooding without analysis can produce mimicry without understanding. Watch for students who can copy but not originate.

## Fuzzy Areas

- How much exposure before analysis? No fixed rule — calibration needed per domain.
- Does this apply to purely conceptual domains (philosophy, math) where there is no sensory component?
- At what point does "I can do it without thinking" mean deep encoding vs. memorized repetition?

## Source

Rick Beato — Lex Fridman Podcast #492

Core observation: Bebop musicians absorbed the language of jazz by living inside it from childhood. Theory came years later — and when it came, they already knew how to play. Analysis was recognition, not instruction.
