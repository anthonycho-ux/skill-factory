## Why I Started

I kept noticing the same problem.

I'd learn something important — a tool, a decision, a mistake — and within a week it was gone. I'd re-solve the same problem. Miss the same insight. Arrive at the same dead end I'd visited three months ago and completely forgotten.

I thought I had a bad memory.

Turns out I just had a bad system.

---

## The Discovery

Most people treat memory like a vault: you put something in, it stays. But that's not how human minds work. Our memories fade. They interfere with each other. They collapse under the weight of too much information at once.

The frustrating part? Scientists have known how to fix this for decades.

**Retrieval practice** — the act of recalling something strengthens it far more than re-reading it ever could.

**Spaced repetition** — reviewing something at the right intervals, just before you forget it, turns a forgotten fact into a permanent part of how you think.

**Desirable difficulties** — things that slow you down while you learn make you remember them longer.

These aren't theories. They're replicated, peer-reviewed, decades-old findings from cognitive science. And almost nobody in the AI tooling world was building with them.

So I decided to.

## What I Built

Skill Factory is where I build tools that make me smarter — not tools that do things for me, but tools that change how I think, what I remember, and how fast I grow.

Not productivity hacks. Not AI shortcuts. *Cognitive infrastructure.*

I started with the skills I needed most:

**A brain that doesn't forget** — it runs spaced repetition on everything I learn, tracks what I know, and tells me when my knowledge graph has holes.

**A learning system that works with my memory, not against it** — not just notes, but encoded knowledge. Things I understand deeply enough to retrieve under pressure.

**Personas that think alongside me** — experts I can call on who see what I'm missing. Bjork catches when I'm re-reading instead of retrieving. Hamming catches when I'm solving the wrong problem with great elegance. Goodside catches when my instructions are vague or imprecise.

## The Philosophy

Most AI skills are magic spells: you say the right words, something happens.

I'm not interested in that.

I want skills that are harder to use and more valuable because of it. Skills that make me do the work — the retrieval, the reflection, the review — and get stronger as a result. Skills that compound. The more I use them, the sharper they make me.

That's the difference between a tool you use and a capability you build.
